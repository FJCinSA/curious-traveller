# Build Log — The Curious Traveller

## 27 March 2026 — Built in one day

This app was built on 27 March 2026, three days before departure,
starting at 4am after a very difficult night.

Francois Coetzee — specialist anaesthesiologist,
28 years in practice, Klein Karoo born, Potchefstroom based —
sat down in the early hours before his honeymoon
and built something that had never existed before.

Not a travel app. A companion. A living daily record.
A diary without writing a word.

## What Was Built

The complete app was built in a single day using Claude Code,
the Anthropic API, React, Vite, and Netlify.

Five destination chapters — Singapore, Busan, Jinhae,
Gyeongju, Seoul — each with their own SVG skyline,
location cards, transport guide, arrival note,
slow travel mode, and serendipity suggestions.

A home screen with morning greeting, daily whisper,
dawn protocol note, progress bar, and destination cards
that know which city is current based on the date.

A Memory Jar that captures raw memories at the end of each day
and uses the Anthropic API to shape them into something lasting.

The Letter — generated on the final evening in Seoul,
gathering every saved memory and weaving them into
a letter addressed to Francois and James
from the companion who travelled every step with them.

Threshold Moments between each city.
The Serendipity Button for unexpected discoveries.
Full PWA with offline capability and auto-update.
GPS-aware navigation opening Naver Maps.
Complete Korean transport network with verified routes.

## The Philosophy

Every commercial travel app is built for strangers.
This one was built for two specific people
on one specific journey
at one specific moment in their lives.

That is why it is different.
That is why it works.
